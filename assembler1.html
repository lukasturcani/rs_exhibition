Imagine that building a molecular cage is like building a lego structure. Molecular precursors are our lego blocks.

In the next steps 2 different types of lego blocks will be defined: Building Blocks (colored in red) and Linkers (colored in blue). Bur first, in order to build a molecular cage you need to select a shape, which represents the framework used to correctly position the Building Blocks and Linkers. The Building Blocks will replace the grey spheres in the shape, while the linkers the grey sticks.

Larger molecular shapes will generate more flexible cages. More flexible cages will have higher chances of not being shape persistent, which means they will lose their internal pore.
