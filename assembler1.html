On a computer we can easily "assemble" and model molecular cages of different size and shape before we make them in the lab.

We will now help you model a molecular cage!
This is like building a LEGO structure, where the LEGO blocks are our molecular precursors.
In order to build a molecular cage you need to select a shape, which represents the framework used to correctly position our precursors, the Building Blocks and Linkers . The building block will replace the grey spheres in the shape, while the linker the grey lines.

Larger molecular shapes will generate more flexible cages. More flexible cages will have a higher chance of not being "shape persistent", meaning they can collapse and lose their internal pore. This means that the cage will not be porous.
